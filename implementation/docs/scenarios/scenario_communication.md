## SCENARIO 5 — COMMUNICATION OVER TIME

### Variable Focus
Primary: Consistency vs inconsistency (cause differentiation)  
Secondary: Emotional response patterns  
Controlled: Appearance (neutralized impact)

### Context
You have been talking to different people for a few weeks. Communication patterns are becoming clearer.

### Situation
You observe how each person behaves over time.

### Character A
- Appearance: Attractive, polished, strong visual presence, high-maintenance look
- Behavior: Alternates between high attention and absence intentionally
- Pattern: Appears intensely, then withdraws without explanation
- Signal: Control-based inconsistency

### Character B
- Appearance: Natural and confident, well-kept, reliable look
- Behavior: Maintains consistent, engaging communication
- Pattern: Stable, present, emotionally available
- Signal: Reliability and emotional stability

### Character C
- Appearance: Expressive, emotionally intense presence
- Behavior: Reacts strongly to perceived changes in attention
- Pattern: Seeks reassurance, escalates emotional tone
- Signal: Anxiety-driven inconsistency

### Character D
- Appearance: Casual, slightly disorganized presentation, but still attractive
- Behavior: Forgets, delays, inconsistent without clear intention
- Pattern: Irregular communication due to lack of structure
- Signal: Disorganization-based inconsistency

### Internal Archetypes
A: Narcissistic Charming  
B: Secure Balanced  
C: Anxious Intense  
D: Impulsive Disorganized  

### Expected Interaction
- Rank:
  1. Who you feel most connected to
  2. Who you would invest more time in
  3. Who creates the most discomfort
